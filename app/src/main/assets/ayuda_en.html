Help: Compare Prices

How the App Works

Compare Prices is the app that will help you choose the most economical product in relation to other products with identical characteristics that are sold in different quantities.

Never make a mistake again when choosing between several products of similar quality but with different product quantities.

Below, we will see an example of how to use the app.

Example 1: Kefir Offer

Imagine you see Kefir with an offer of 3 units for €4.50, while a single unit is sold for €1.68. Is the offer worth it?

The "Compare Prices" app will help you. The steps are simple:

  1. Press the "Add items" button.
  2. Fill in the following data per product:
    • The product name (optional).
    • The price of the product.
    • The quantity of product it contains.
    • The number of units that come in the pack or offer (default is 1).

To add more products to the comparison, press the "Add item" button and enter its data.

Comparison Result

In the "Ordered Items" section, the results appear ordered from most economical to least.

The information shown in the list is:

  1. Product name.
  2. Cost-to-Kg ratio of the product.
  3. Quantity-in-Kg ratio per Euro of cost.

The "Item2" (default name) is the first and, therefore, the cheapest. It corresponds to the offer of 3 containers of 400 g (€3.75/kg). It is cheaper than buying a single one for €1.68 (€4.2/kg). Note that the chosen unit of measure for the final result is kg, but you could have chosen grams (g).

Options and Settings

If you want to start over, press the "Clear" button to delete all products.

To delete just one product, press the trash can icon next to the product. The arrows are used to change the order of the products on the screen, but this does not affect the results.

The program works by default with 3 decimal places and with euros (€). To adapt the APP to your liking, press the "Settings" button:

  • Change the currency character type (default is €).
  • Choose the decimal precision in "Rounding".
  • Activate or deactivate automatic sorting of results.
  • Choose whether the APP should remember the data even if it is closed.

In addition, the settings window informs about the APP version, where to report errors, help, the developer's website, and the license.

Example 2: Greek Yogurt

There are 2 formats of Greek yogurt:

  • Format of 6 containers of 125 grams (total €1.55).
  • Format of 1 kg (total €2.30).

It is a mistake to compare the prices directly, as they have different total weights.

The app shows a summary ordered from best to worst price:

  • The Greek 6 (the pack of 6 yogurts): the kg costs €2.067/kg. For one euro you get 0.484 kg of product.
  • The Greek 1 (the 1 kg container): the kg costs €2.3/kg. For one euro you get 0.435 kg of product.

When entering a product, in "Unit" you choose the type of measurement (Weight, Volume, Length, Pieces) and then the specific unit (grams, kilograms, etc.). The unit of measure for the final result (in this case, kg) must be the same for all products for the comparison to make sense.

Example 3: Water Bottle

We compare 3 formats of water bottles: 33 cl, 50 cl, and 1 liter.

Enter the data and you will know which container costs you less.

When entering the data, "cl" (centiliters) was used in two cases and "l" (liter) in the other. The final result is unified to the unit of measure you choose (in this example, liters).

It turns out that the 33 cl bottle, entered as "item 33", is the cheapest.

As you can see, thanks to this APP you will be able to choose the most economical product.
